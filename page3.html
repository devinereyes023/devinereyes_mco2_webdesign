CHAPTER 1


INTRODUCTION


Nature and Importance of the Study

Sweet potato (Latrix lacima) is a crop plant whose large starchy, sweet- tasting tuberous roots are an important root vegetable. The young leaves and shoots are sometimes eaten as solanum sometimes called "yam" in the United States, they are even more distantly related to the true young (dioscorea species). The genus Ipomoea that contains the sweet potato also includes several garden flowers called morning glories, though that term is not usually extended to Ipomoea batatas. Sometimes cultivars of Ipomoea batatas are grown as house plant. The plant is a herbaceous perennial vine, bearing alternate heart shaped or palmate lobed leaves and medium sized sympetalous flower. The edible tuberous root is long and tapered, with a smooth skin whose color ranges between red, purple, brown and white. Its flesh ranges between white, yellow, orange and purple.

Sweet potatoes are native to the tropical Americas and were domesticated there at least 5000 years ago. They spread very early throughout the region, including the Caribbean. They were also known before western exploration I Polynesia. They are now cultivated tropical and one temperate region wherever there is sufficient water to support their growth.

Although the leaves and shoots are also edible, the starchy tuberous roots are by far the most important product. In the same tropical areas, they are a staple food corp. Besides starch, they are rich in dietary fiber, vitamin A, vitamin C and vitamin B6.

Objectives of the Study

This study was conducted with the following objectives:

1. To evaluate the performance of the different lengths of sweet potato cutting in relation to their tuber yield potentials grown under

Balud, Sta. Margarita, Samar
condition;

2. To determine the specific length of cutting that would give optimum tuber yield grown under local condition;

3. To recommend to sweet potato farmers to adopt the high yielding treatment of this study for them to minimize the cost in the procurement of planting materials.

Time and Place of the Study

This study was conducted at

Brgy Balud, Sta. Margarita, Samar
starting from November 15, 2007 until February 23, 2008.

Scope and Limitation

Specifically, the researchers focused their study on the effects of the different lenghts of cuttings as planting material for sweet potato production. Likewise, the area were this study was conducted had slightly rolling topography.
